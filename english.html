Swing Spring 15

What

The biggest and best Lindy Hop dance camp in Finland, Swing Spring is here again!

When

May 13th - 17th 2015.

Where

The camp will take place at Kanneljärven Opisto, Lohja!

Register

Registeration opens in January 2015.

Program

Come join us at Lohja in May 2015, when all Finnish Lindy Hoppers meet at Swing Spring 2015! Our amazing teachers will make you learn and enjoy dancing, while live music and DJs will make you dance and swing on evening parties.

Classes

Swing Spring is a Lindy Hop camp, meaning that Lindy Hop will be taught daily. In addition to Lindy, we will have elective classes and tasters on other dances or subjects, so that they promote the general idea of the camp.

Parties

There will be total of four nights of dancing during Swing Spring 2015, starting from Wednesday until Saturday! During this time we'll also have exciting competitions and performances.

Competitions

Two different lindy hop competitions will be arranged during Swing Spring 2015. Both of them are improvised social dance contests so no choreographies or special skills are required. Competition entry is included in the prices of the camp and party tickets, though a qualifying round may be arranged in the early evening before the party starts. The best way to register for the competitions is to do it while registering for the event.

Cabaret

On Saturday night there will be a cabaret. All kinds of performances are welcome to the cabaret, please, contact Tiiti (tiiti.valonen(at)comets.fi).

Teachers

Riikka Antikainen

Riikka Antikainen has been a happy Lindy Hopper since 2006. Over the years, along came Authentic Jazz, Balboa, teaching and other sorts of swing-connected craziness both in Finland and abroad. Today, Riikka teaches Lindy Hop and Balboa through Rock'n'Swing Club Comets ry in Helsinki. She loves social dancing, DJing and teaching people to dance in rhythm, in control and together.

Anni Heikkinen

Anni Heikkinen is a swing dance teacher from Oulu. Her full-time job is teaching future dance teachers at the Oulu University of Applied Sciences. For Anni, swing music and Lindy Hop are unending sources of inspiration and self expression.

Teemu Hirsimäki

Teemu Hirsimäki got into dancing in 2002, first in the form of Finnish traditional dances and Tap dancing before finding Lindy Hop in 2010. During the last years Teemu has actively participated in swing dance workshops and taught Lindy Hop in Helsinki. According to Teemu the best elements of Lindy Hop are working together with your partner, natural body movement and interpreting inspiring Jazz music.

Adam LaMontagne

Adam is a club-dancer turned lindy hopper from the mean streets of Boston, Massachusetts. He has been dancing and teaching swing for over 10 years. Adam co-founded the Lindy League of Western Massachusetts swing dance organization, Crazylegs and Wicked Nines performance groups and he is the man behind lindyhop.fi. Since 2010 Adam has been dancing and traveling around Finland and all over Europe to learn more, dance more and share the love of swing dancing.

Sanna Leinonen

Since 2007 Sanna has been intensively traveling for dancing lindy hop and balboa. Sanna's exceptional abilities in perception and analysis and her analytical mind make her a sought teacher for groups and private lessons. She shines the light from within reflecting her love for the lightheartedness, gaiety and cheekiness of this dance.

Minna Marttila

During the 90′s, Minna Marttila had to take a break from Judo and during that time fell in love with swing dancing. And she never looked back. Her journey in the swing world started with Boogie Woogie soon followed by Lindy Hop and later on by the elegant but spicy Balboa. She is the proud mother of the ten years old “Puistotanssit – Lindy in the Park” in her home town Helsinki. Promoting social dancing is close to her heart.

Mikko Multanen

Mikko Multanen fell in love with Lindy Hop 2005 and a year later also with Balboa and Authentic Jazz. He has taught Lindy Hop, Balboa and Authentic Jazz since 2007. Mikko loves the freedom and happiness of Lindy Hop and enjoys teaching people about rhythm and connection.

Aleksis Nokso-Koivisto

Aleksis Nokso-Koivisto is one of the most well-known Lindy Hoppers in Finland. Since 1995, he’s danced most Rock’n’Swing dances and other dances as well, but his dancing and teaching is also inspired by music and other performing arts. His classes are above all physical, because the only way to learn to dance is to dance. However, this must be done with thought: deliberate practice is the only way to mastery.

Heidi Pohjola

Heidi Pohjola started her dancing career with ballroom and latin dances in 1995 and she competed in them for well over ten years. Round 2000, she also found Finnish social dancing and Rock’n’Swing dances, in which she also wanted to compete, leading to three Finnish Lindy Hop championships. Nowadays, Heidi is mostly retired from competing, but she continues teaching and social dancing.

Saku Päivärinta

Saku Päivärinta swing dancer from Helsinki. At the moment, Saku teaches Lindy Hop and Balboa mainly in Helsinki. Saku’s dance program also includes Tap dancing. His trademarks are energetic dancing and a love for social dancing.

Suvi Ravela

Suvi Ravela started dancing to balance out her studying. During her dancing career, she has danced Flamenco, Ballet and modern African dancing, before finally finding Lindy Hop, which she started teaching in Helsinki in 2012. Suvi loves Lindy Hop because of the music, the rhythm, the communication and creating the dance together with your partner.
